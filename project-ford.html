OVERVIEW:

FordPass: From Connected Ownership to an EV-Ready Digital Platform

FordPass was created as Ford's primary digital ownership platform, initially focused on connected services for internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles. I worked closely on the FordPass product from FordPass 1.0 through the integration of Ford's first mass-market EV with the Mustang Mach-E, helping evolve both the experience design and the creative identity of the app.

My role spanned experience strategy, UI design, and design direction, including leading the visual language and branding of the new FordPass app while ensuring it authentically extended Ford's brand into a modern digital product.

The Mach-E launch marked a turning point: Ford was no longer just adding features, it was redefining ownership through software.

WHAT WAS DONE
  • Establish trust as a daily-use ownership companion
  • Simplify complex vehicle data and controls to a user friendly design language
  • Work across a fragmented ICE/EV vehicle ecosystems
  • Feel unmistakably "Ford" while still modern and digital-first
THE WORK
  • Platform-First UI Direction
    Designed FordPass as a vehicle-aware platform, not separate ICE and EV experiences
    Shared layouts and navigation with conditional modules based on vehicle capabilities
    Preserved familiarity while enabling deeper EV-specific functionality
  • Shift from Status UI → Progress & State UI
    Moved beyond static "current state" screens
    Introduced progress indicators and time-based feedback
    Reoriented hierarchy toward what happens next, not just what is now
  • Energy as a Primary Visual Element
    Reframed range and charge as confidence indicators, not raw data
    Prioritized clarity and reassurance over technical precision
    Used hierarchy, contrast, and language to reduce EV-related anxiety
  • Charging as a Core Experience
    Elevated charging from a secondary feature to a primary surface
    Designed clear visual states for:
    • Charging vs not charging
    • Home vs public charging
    • Time to meaningful milestones
  • Progressive Disclosure for New EV Owners
    Simple default views with expandable advanced details
    Human language over engineering terminology
    Designed for confidence on day one, mastery over time
  • Visual System Evolution
    Refined typography and hierarchy for data-dense screens
    Used motion and state change to reinforce system feedback
    Maintained brand consistency while supporting new interaction models
GALLERY
FORD WORK

As complexity increased, the system relied on progressive disclosure rather than additional modes or screens. Default views remained simple and glanceable, deeper detail was available when needed, and the visual system evolved to support denser information without feeling technical or overwhelming.

The result was an experience that evolved in capability without feeling redesigned allowing FordPass to support Ford's first EV launch inside a single, coherent interface.
